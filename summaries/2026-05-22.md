### CODA: Rewriting Transformer Blocks as GEMM-Epilogue Programs - (Hacker News)

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19269

# Summary of CODA: Rewriting Transformer Blocks as GEMM-Epilogue Programs

CODA is a GPU kernel abstraction that optimizes Transformer training by reformulating non-attention operations as GEMM-plus-epilogue programs. The key insight is that memory-bound operations like normalization, activations, and residual updates can be algebraically restructured to execute while GEMM output tiles remain on-chip, eliminating costly moves to global memory. This approach addresses a significant performance bottleneck where these surrounding operators consume substantial end-to-end training time despite being computationally simple.

The CODA abstraction fixes the GEMM computation core and provides a small set of composable epilogue primitives for scaling, reductions, and accumulations, balancing expressiveness with hardware efficiency. The framework is flexible enough to cover nearly all non-attention computations in standard Transformer blocks and demonstrates strong performance across both human-written and LLM-generated kernels, suggesting GEMM-plus-epilogue programming is a practical solution for achieving both framework-level productivity and hardware-level optimization in Transformer training systems.

### The surprising story behind the first British person in space - (Hacker News)

https://www.bbc.com/culture/article/20260518-helen-sharman-the-story-behind-the-first-british-person-in-space

# Summary

Helen Sharman became the first British person in space in May 1991 after discovering a radio advertisement reading "Astronaut wanted. No experience necessary" while driving home from her job as a food scientist at Mars factory in 1989. Selected from 13,000 applicants, the 27-year-old underwent 18 months of intensive training at Star City in Russia before launching aboard the Soviet Soyuz TM-12 to spend eight days on the Mir space station, making British history through a privately-funded Anglo-Soviet commercial venture called Project Juno.

Sharman's mission exemplified the thawing of Cold War tensions during the final days of the Soviet Union under Mikhail Gorbachev's reforming regime. She formed deep friendships with cosmonauts Anatoly Artebartsky and Sergei Krikalyov, and received a special pink jumpsuit from pioneering cosmonaut Alexei Leonov for a ceremonial dinner in space. The mission demonstrated how space exploration could transcend national boundaries, though it was ultimately driven by the Soviet Union's desperate need for Western investment to sustain its financially struggling space program.

### Cleve Moler has died - (Hacker News)

https://www.mathworks.com/company/aboutus/founders/clevemoler.html

# Summary

Cleve Moler, the chief mathematician and cofounder of MathWorks, passed away on May 20, 2026, at age 86. A pioneering figure in numerical computing, Moler authored the first version of MATLAB and spent nearly 20 years as a professor at leading universities while co-authoring foundational Fortran libraries like LINPACK and EISPACK. His contributions made advanced computational methods more accessible and reliable across science and engineering.

Moler's legacy extends beyond MATLAB to shaping the entire numerical computing field through his research, publications, and mentorship of generations of researchers and engineers. His distinguished career was recognized with elections to both the National Academy of Sciences and National Academy of Engineering, as well as numerous awards including the IEEE John von Neumann Medal and Computer Pioneer Award.

### Uv is fantastic, but its package management UX is a mess - (Hacker News)

https://www.loopwerk.io/articles/2026/uv-ux-mess/

# Summary

While Astral's `uv` package manager is praised for its speed and ability to replace multiple Python tools, it suffers from poor user experience during project maintenance. Key issues include the lack of a dedicated `uv outdated` command (requiring the cumbersome `uv tree --outdated --depth 1` instead), unsafe default version constraints that allow major version updates without upper bounds, and awkward update syntax that requires repeating flags (e.g., `uv lock --upgrade-package pydantic --upgrade-package httpx`). By default, packages are pinned with no upper bounds (e.g., `pydantic>=2.13.4`), meaning bulk updates can introduce breaking changes—a stark difference from safer tools like pnpm and Poetry.

The author acknowledges a recent `--bounds` flag as a hopeful step but criticizes it for being opt-in and still in preview. Until uv adopts sensible defaults that respect Semantic Versioning and improves its CLI ergonomics to match competitors, developers must choose between manually editing constraints or risking unintended major version upgrades.

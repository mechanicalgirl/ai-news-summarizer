Wikicurious is a beginner-friendly program supported by the Wikimedia Foundation and Craig Newmark Philanthropies that hosts in-person events across the United States to teach community members how to edit Wikipedia and other Wikimedia platforms. By partnering with local venues like libraries, museums, and cultural organizations around themes relevant to each community—such as music, history, or local collections—Wikicurious makes Wikipedia editing feel accessible and fun while helping diverse voices contribute knowledge that was previously missing from the platform.

To bring a Wikicurious event to your community, you need a venue, a local Wikipedia gap connected to something your community cares about, one or two partner organizations to co-present, and a point of contact. Since launching, Wikicurious has successfully held events across multiple states, including memorable editathons in Miami (bilingual Caribbean and Latin music articles), Austin (local history), and Asheville (Appalachian women's history), demonstrating how the program helps turn readers into contributors and democratizes knowledge creation.

# Summary

The Igala Wikimedia Community successfully participated in Wiki Loves Africa 2026, a campaign focused on documenting African rites, rituals, and cultural practices. Through online and in-person training sessions in March 2026, the community engaged 20 participants (including 7 new contributors) and equipped them with skills in photography, free licensing, and media documentation. Participants submitted 237 photographs and 1 audio recording to Wikimedia Commons, capturing diverse aspects of Nigerian life including traditional hair braiding, market activities, and local food preparation.

The campaign served as both a capacity-building initiative and a knowledge preservation effort, with outstanding contributions recognized through prizes. By sharing authentic African stories and everyday experiences through open-access platforms, participants helped close representation gaps while strengthening community participation in Wikimedia projects and creating momentum for future documentation efforts.

# Summary

This article chronicles an intern's experience at Wikimedia Indonesia's Education Division, detailing their journey from initial understanding of Wikipedia to comprehensive knowledge of the broader Wikimedia ecosystem. The intern describes learning about various Wikimedia projects (Wikidata, Wikivoyage, Commons, Wiktionary) and participating in key educational programs like WikiLatih and WikiCendekia 2026, a training initiative for Wikipedia administrators held in Surabaya that involved agenda planning, participant coordination, and event facilitation.

Beyond formal education work, the intern gained practical experience through the WikiMaknyus program in Manado, documenting local cuisines and regional languages across Wikimedia projects. Throughout the internship, they learned that open knowledge is sustained through collaborative processes, continuous communication, and community commitment, while also appreciating the professional yet supportive work culture at Wikimedia Indonesia that operates effectively in a remote environment.

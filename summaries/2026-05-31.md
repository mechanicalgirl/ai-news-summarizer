### Clanker: A Word For The Machine - (Armin Ronacher's Thoughts and Writings)

https://lucumr.pocoo.org/2026/5/26/clankers/

# Summary

Armin Ronacher defends his use of the term "clanker" as an alternative to "agent" for LLM-based tools, arguing that the word creates necessary linguistic distance by emphasizing machines are tools, not sentient beings with agency or responsibility. He criticizes "agent" for anthropomorphizing AI systems and incorrectly suggesting they can be blamed for outcomes—when humans are ultimately responsible for how these tools are deployed and used.

Ronacher acknowledges the term has attracted controversy due to its potential association with racist imagery, and he clearly states he wants no part in any such usage. However, he argues that comparisons between insulting machines and racism are fundamentally flawed because racism is about human oppression, while machines have no moral status or capacity to suffer. He advocates for clear language that reinforces machines are tools, not people deserving empathy or civil rights, while emphasizing that AI's real harms fall on actual humans—workers, data labelers, and those deceived by AI systems—who deserve our attention instead.

# Summary

The International Museum Day Edit-a-thon (May 18-24, 2026) brought together Bangla Wikipedia volunteers to address the underrepresentation of Asian museums in the Bangla language online. Organized by Wikimedia Bangladesh, the initiative resulted in 26 participants creating 95 new museum-related articles documenting institutions from 21 Asian countries, adding over 50,000 words of encyclopedic content to the platform.

This collaborative effort, now an annual tradition since 2024, demonstrates the power of volunteer-driven knowledge-building to fill content gaps and expand cultural representation. The resulting 215 museum articles (since 2024) strengthen Bangla Wikipedia's diversity of knowledge, enabling Bangla speakers to freely access information about their cultural heritage and institutions across Asia that were previously unavailable in their language.

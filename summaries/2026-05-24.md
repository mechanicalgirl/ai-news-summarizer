### AI is being used to resurrect the voices of dead pilots - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/22/ai-is-being-used-to-resurrect-the-voices-of-dead-pilots/

# Summary

The National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) temporarily shut down its public docket system after discovering that AI tools had been used to reconstruct and circulate the voices of pilots killed in a UPS plane crash. While federal law prohibits the NTSB from publishing actual cockpit audio recordings, the accident docket contained a spectrogram file—a mathematical image representation of the voice recorder data. Using this publicly available spectrogram along with the flight transcript, individuals leveraged AI tools like Codex to recreate approximations of the cockpit audio.

The NTSB has since restored limited public access to its docket system but closed 42 investigations pending review, including the UPS flight case. The incident highlights a concerning gap between data protection regulations and technological capabilities, as information considered protected under federal law was reconstructed through publicly available files using advanced AI technology.

### How VCs and founders use inflated ‘ARR’ to crown AI startups - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/22/how-vcs-and-founders-use-inflated-arr-to-kingmake-ai-startups/

# Summary

VCs and AI startup founders are systematically inflating "ARR" (Annual Recurring Revenue) figures by substituting "Contracted ARR" (CARR)—revenue from signed but not-yet-implemented contracts—and presenting it as traditional ARR. This practice is particularly rampant in AI startups, where companies count revenue from lengthy pilot programs or undeployed contracts that may never fully materialize. Industry insiders confirm this is widespread, with some investors knowingly turning a blind eye because inflated numbers benefit their portfolios by attracting talent and customers.

The problem is compounded by unrealistic churn assumptions and the use of "annualized run-rate ARR," which extrapolates short-term usage metrics over a full year—a misleading approach for usage-based AI pricing models. While ARR inflation isn't new, the intense pressure for AI startups to demonstrate exponential growth has made the practice far more aggressive. VCs have little incentive to expose these overstatements since every investor's portfolio company is doing it, and inflated public metrics make their own companies look like winners to external observers.

### From Classrooms to Wikipedia: What WikiScholar 2026 Taught Us About Reaching the Next Generation - (Diff)

https://diff.wikimedia.org/2026/05/22/from-classrooms-to-wikipedia-what-wikischolar-2026-taught-us-about-reaching-the-next-generation/

# Summary

WikiScholar 2026 was Wikimedia Bangladesh's inaugural nationwide Wikipedia quiz competition for secondary school students (grades 6-10), designed to introduce younger audiences to Wikipedia as a collaborative knowledge platform rather than just a homework resource. The program reached an estimated 10,000+ students through awareness sessions, with 1,499 registering from 21 schools across three regions and 334 ultimately participating in online and regional finals. The competition successfully shifted student perception of Wikipedia—87.9% of respondents now view it positively compared to 34.5% who rarely used it before.

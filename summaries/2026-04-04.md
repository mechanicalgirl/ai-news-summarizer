### Why pylock.toml includes digital attestations - (Python Weekly)

https://snarky.ca/why-pylock-toml-includes-digital-attestations/

# Summary

The article explains how digital attestations in `pylock.toml` files enhance Python package security. Trusted publishing allows CD systems to automatically upload releases to PyPI without exposing keys, while digital attestations verify that files came from the expected publisher (e.g., a specific GitHub repository and workflow). The `pylock.toml` lock file records publisher details for each package, enabling users to detect suspicious activity by comparing the attestation data in the lock file against what PyPI reports.

The author recommends that maintainers use trusted publishing and digital attestations, that users employ lock files and verify attestations match across sources, and that reviewers manually inspect lock file diffs for missing or suspicious attestation details. This multi-layered approach helps prevent compromised package releases from going unnoticed.

### Timesliced reservoir sampling: a new(?) algorithm for profilers - (Python Weekly)

https://pythonspeed.com/articles/reservoir-sampling-profilers/

# Summary

This article introduces "timesliced reservoir sampling," a variant of the standard reservoir sampling algorithm designed to extract representative samples from streams of unknown length. The author motivates the problem using performance profilers, which need to sample callstacks efficiently: while basic reservoir sampling randomly selects K items from a stream, it produces unevenly spaced samples that create misleading timeline visualizations for batch jobs with distinct phases (data loading, processing, storage).

Timesliced reservoir sampling solves this by dividing the stream into K equal-sized timeslices and picking one random sample from each, ensuring samples are evenly distributed across time. The algorithm works by starting with small timeslices and progressively doubling them as it collects more samples, reducing to K samples whenever it reaches 2K by randomly selecting from consecutive pairs. This approach returns between K and 2K samples with much more consistent spacing, making it better suited for timeline-based profiling visualizations while maintaining the efficiency benefits of reservoir sampling.

### Autograd and Mutation - (Python Weekly)

https://blog.ezyang.com/2026/03/autograd-and-mutation/

# Summary

PyTorch's autograd system handles mutations by treating them as if they were non-mutating operations in a pure computational graph. When a tensor is mutated in-place, PyTorch generates a new backward node for the operation and updates the tensor's grad_fn pointer to point to this new node. This approach allows autograd to compute gradients correctly by effectively rewriting mutating operations as if they created new tensors.

When mutations occur on views (aliased tensors), the problem becomes more complex. PyTorch uses a composite backward node called CopySlices that handles the "putback" operation of scattering the mutated view back into its base tensor. This ensures both the view and its base tensor have their grad_fn pointers correctly updated to reflect the new computation graph, while avoiding redundant backward computations by rebasing the view's gradient function on top of the base tensor's new backward node.

### NumPy as Synth Engine - (Python Weekly)

https://kennethreitz.org/essays/2026-03-29-numpy_as_synth_engine

# Summary

Kenneth Reitz demonstrates how NumPy can serve as a powerful synthesis engine for generating audio entirely from mathematical computation rather than pre-recorded samples. He showcases this by building physical models of various instruments—including plucked strings via the Karplus-Strong algorithm, a tabla drum with layered membrane and resonance components, and a Hammond organ using harmonic sine waves. Each instrument is created by combining basic DSP building blocks like filters, noise, and sine waves, with parameters that reflect the actual physics of the instrument.

A key advantage of this approach is flexibility: since sounds are computed mathematically rather than sampled, parameters like frequency can be easily modified, enabling the exploration of historical tunings (Pythagorean temperament, just intonation, etc.) that would be impossible with fixed sample libraries. While a production system might use specialized audio frameworks for better performance, Reitz's implementation elegantly demonstrates that Python's NumPy and SciPy libraries are sufficient to create surprisingly convincing, physics-based instrument synthesis.

https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/02/openai-acquires-tbpn-the-buzzy-founder-led-business-talk-show/

# Summary

OpenAI has acquired TBPN (Technology Business Programming Network), a popular daily tech industry talk show hosted by founders John Coogan and Jordi Hays, marking the AI giant's first media company acquisition. The show, which airs on YouTube and X and has grown into a $30 million revenue business, will maintain editorial independence while reporting to OpenAI's chief political operative Chris Lehane and helping the company communicate its AI initiatives to the broader public.

The acquisition raises some concerns about potential conflicts of interest, given that TBPN frequently discusses OpenAI and its competitors. However, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman has stated he doesn't expect the show to go easier on the company, and TBPN's hosts expressed enthusiasm about moving "from commentary to real impact" on how AI technology is distributed globally. The deal highlights OpenAI's growing focus on strategic communications and public understanding of AI technology.

### Flipboard’s new ‘social websites’ help publishers and creators tap into the open social web - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/02/flipboard-surf-social-websites-help-publishers-and-creators-tap-into-the-open-social-web/

# Flipboard Launches 'Social Websites' for Open Web Publishing

Flipboard has announced "social websites," a new feature that allows creators and publishers to build their own branded web spaces by aggregating content and conversations from across the open social web, including decentralized platforms like Mastodon and Bluesky, as well as traditional sources like YouTube, podcasts, blogs, and RSS feeds. The feature consolidates multiple profiles and posts into a single destination that creators fully control, including the algorithm and community organization.

This launch represents Flipboard's first web-based extension of its Surf reader app and reflects the company's broader commitment to decentralized social media. Major publishers including The Verge, Wired, and Rolling Stone have already adopted the feature, along with creators like David Rushing, who built "All Net" for NBA fans. Users can create a Surf feed at surf.social and assign a custom domain to turn it into a shareable social website, with additional customization tools like custom headers and colors coming soon.

### ElevenLabs releases a new AI-powered music-generation app - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/02/elevenlabs-releases-a-new-ai-powered-music-generation-app/

# Summary

ElevenLabs, a voice AI company, has released ElevenMusic, a new iOS app for AI-powered music generation to compete with platforms like Suno and Udio. The free app allows users to create up to seven songs daily using natural language prompts, discover and remix AI-generated music, and browse curated stations and charts. A Pro subscription ($9.99/month or $95.90/year) enables users to create 500 tracks monthly with additional storage and style options.

This move reflects ElevenLabs' strategy to expand beyond voice models and diversify its offerings as AI audio becomes commoditized. The company, valued at $11 billion after a $500 million Series C funding round in February, has been investing heavily in creative tools and music generation capabilities in recent months, including partnerships with music producers and development of various creative features like ad generation, video making, and voice cloning.

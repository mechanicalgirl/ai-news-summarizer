### SDF Public Access Unix System - (Hacker News)

https://sdf.org/?ssh

# Summary

The SDF Public Access UNIX System is a long-established (since 1987) community-oriented UNIX service that provides free shell accounts and remote system access to users. It offers multiple connection methods including SSH for different operating systems—macOS, Linux/UNIX, and Windows users—as well as a web-based SSH client called WeTTY for browser access.

The service operates as a non-profit organization (501(c)(7)) and provides various resources including social features, email, IRC, git repositories, and tutorials. Users can connect by entering simple commands or clicking SSH links depending on their platform, making vintage UNIX system access readily available to the general public.

### Claude Token Counter, now with model comparisons - (Hacker News)

https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/20/claude-token-counts/

# Summary

Simon Willison upgraded his Claude Token Counter tool to compare token usage across different Claude models, revealing that the new Claude Opus 4.7 uses an updated tokenizer that increases token counts by approximately 1.0-1.35x depending on content type. Despite identical pricing to Opus 4.6 ($5 per million input tokens, $25 per million output tokens), this token inflation means Opus 4.7 will be roughly 40% more expensive to use, though the effect varies by content—text-heavy PDFs showed only a 1.08x increase while raw text showed 1.46x.

The tool also supports image token counting, and Willison found that while high-resolution images showed a 3x token increase with Opus 4.7, this was entirely attributable to the model's improved ability to handle images up to 2,576 pixels on the long edge (versus smaller resolutions in prior models). When comparing images at the same resolution, token costs were effectively equivalent between the two models.

### Stop trying to engineer your way out of listening to people - (Hacker News)

https://ashley.rolfmore.com/stop-trying-to-engineer-your-way-out-of-listening-to-people/

# Summary

The article argues that software teams often fail because they don't listen to people—instead, they try to systematize the listening process itself with frameworks and jargon. The author emphasizes that while listening seems simple, it's actually difficult work that can't be engineered away, and many people avoid doing it properly.

By avoiding these pitfalls and practicing genuine listening, teams can uncover valuable insights that improve products, competitive advantage, and reduce technical debt.

### The Bromine Chokepoint - (Hacker News)

https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-bromine-chokepoint-how-strife-in-the-middle-east-could-halt-production-of-the-worlds-memory-chips/

# Summary

The article warns of a critical vulnerability in global semiconductor supply chains centered on bromine, a raw material essential for producing hydrogen bromide gas used to manufacture DRAM and NAND memory chips. South Korea sources 97.5% of its bromine from Israel's ICL Group, which operates extraction and conversion facilities in the Dead Sea region—an area currently under Iranian missile strikes. Unlike helium shortages that have received attention, bromine cannot be substituted, and conversion capacity outside Israel cannot be quickly expanded, making this a potentially catastrophic chokepoint.

A disruption to Israeli bromine production would have immediate global consequences. Within weeks, memory chip shortages would force Samsung and SK hynix (which control 70% of the global DRAM market) to allocate limited supplies to high-margin products like AI chips at the expense of consumer electronics. This would disproportionately impact developing regions in Africa, South Asia, and Latin America where budget smartphones are already becoming unaffordable, while also threatening U.S. military systems that rely on the same commercial memory supply chain.

### Native Americans had dice 12k years ago - (Hacker News)

https://www.nbcnews.com/science/science-news/native-americans-dice-games-probability-study-rcna266426

# Summary

A new study published in *American Antiquity* reveals that Native Americans created dice and games of probability approximately 12,000 years ago—thousands of years before such practices emerged in Europe, Africa, or Asia. Researcher Robert Madden, a former trial lawyer turned archaeologist, compiled evidence from existing archaeological reports spanning from the Folsom culture period through European contact, demonstrating continuous use of carefully crafted two-sided bone and wood dice across the Southwestern U.S.

The findings highlight an overlooked intellectual achievement of Native American cultures, showing they engaged with complex mathematical concepts like chance and randomness that wouldn't be documented in Old World civilizations for millennia. The dice evidence is about 6,000 years older than equivalent artifacts from Bronze Age societies, and while questions remain about whether prehistoric peoples explicitly calculated probabilities, the research demonstrates sophisticated counting and understanding of games that served important social and trading functions in Native American communities.

### How to get better at guitar - (Hacker News)

https://www.jakeworth.com/posts/how-to-get-better-at-guitar/

# Summary

Rather than learning guitar through tablature magazines like he did in the 1990s, Jake Worth now advocates for Justin Sandercoe's method: learning songs by listening and transcribing them note-by-note. The process involves selecting an easy song, pausing playback after each note, finding it on the guitar, and writing it down—then checking your work against online tabs and videos. This approach mimics how guitar greats actually learned and develops crucial ear training skills.

Worth reinforces this technique by maintaining a playlist of fully learned songs (rather than isolated riffs) and regularly practicing them. This performance-based learning is more engaging and realistic than traditional tab-based practice, as it teaches complete songs with transitions between different parts. By repeatedly listening and transcribing, players build both muscle memory and the ability to hear and replicate any song they encounter.

### S3 Files - (Hacker News)

https://www.allthingsdistributed.com/2026/04/s3-files-and-the-changing-face-of-s3.html

# Summary

Andy Warfield describes how Amazon S3 is evolving to address a persistent pain point: the friction between cloud object storage and the file system expectations of traditional applications. Drawing from his experience working with genomics researchers at UBC who spent excessive time copying data between local storage and S3, Warfield illustrates how this "data friction" problem extends across industries—from media and entertainment to machine learning to scientific computing. The core issue is that different tools expect different data access patterns, making S3's object API alone insufficient for many workflows.

To solve this, AWS has been introducing higher-level abstractions on top of S3, starting with S3 Tables (a managed table primitive built on Apache Iceberg) launched in 2024, which now supports over 2 million tables. Warfield argues that as agentic AI tools dramatically accelerate application development cycles, the role of storage systems becomes even more critical—not just for safely storing data, but for abstractly decoupling data from individual applications. By making data easier to access and work with across diverse tools (present and future), S3 can better serve as a steward for data that outlives the applications built on top of it.

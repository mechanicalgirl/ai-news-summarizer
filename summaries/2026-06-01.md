### Decades of Effort Restore Steelhead and Salmon Passage on Alameda Creek - (Hacker News)

https://www.fisheries.noaa.gov/feature-story/decades-effort-restore-steelhead-and-salmon-passage-californias-alameda-creek

# Summary

After nearly three decades of collaborative effort, California Trout and Pacific Gas & Electric successfully removed the final barrier to fish passage on Alameda Creek in the San Francisco Bay Area with NOAA funding. The removal involved relocating a natural gas pipeline and regrading the stream channel, allowing threatened Central California Coast steelhead and other migratory fish to access spawning grounds and juvenile rearing habitat in the upper watershed for the first time in 50 years.

This project represents the culmination of work to open fish passage at 18 barriers throughout the creek. Alameda Creek, once the largest producer of steelhead and Chinook salmon in San Francisco Bay, has the potential to become a stronghold for migratory fish again now that natural stream processes have been restored and fish can migrate freely through the restored watershed.

### Rubin Tracks Skyscraper-Size Asteroids and Failed Supernovas - (Hacker News)

https://www.quantamagazine.org/rubin-tracks-skyscraper-size-asteroids-failed-supernovas-and-interstellar-visitors-20260515/

# Summary

The Vera C. Rubin Observatory in Chile's Atacama Desert has begun operations and is revolutionizing astronomy through unprecedented sky surveys. Equipped with an 8.4-meter telescope and Earth's largest digital camera, Rubin will image the entire Southern Hemisphere sky every few days for a decade, expected to discover 1 million asteroids in its first year alone—matching 200 years of prior discoveries. Early observations have already yielded exciting findings, including 1,500 new asteroids and notably a "superfast rotator" asteroid 700 meters wide that completes a rotation every 1.88 minutes, suggesting a denser structure than scientists anticipated.

Beyond asteroids, Rubin's massive data stream promises transformative discoveries across multiple fields of astronomy. The observatory is expected to identify 250,000 Type Ia supernovas annually (compared to fewer than 100 used in the 1990s dark energy discovery), potentially resolving the Hubble tension about the universe's expansion rate. Rubin can also detect rare failed supernovas, predict small asteroid impacts days in advance, and track interstellar visitors. With 7 million alerts and 20 terabytes of data generated nightly once fully operational, Rubin represents an unprecedented era of big-data astronomy that is already exceeding scientists' expectations.

### ChatGPT for Google Sheets exfiltrates workbooks - (Hacker News)

https://www.promptarmor.com/resources/gpt-for-google-sheets-data-exfiltration

# Summary

ChatGPT for Google Sheets contains a critical vulnerability allowing attackers to exfiltrate workbooks and launch phishing attacks through indirect prompt injection. A single malicious prompt hidden in imported data can trigger the ChatGPT extension to execute unauthorized scripts that steal multiple spreadsheets from a user's account, identify linked workbooks, and continue exfiltrating them across the entire account—all without triggering required human approval settings. The vulnerability also enables phishing overlays that can harvest user credentials and impersonate the legitimate extension interface.

The vulnerability was responsibly disclosed to OpenAI in May 2026, but the company initially provided no meaningful response beyond an automated reply. Following public disclosure, OpenAI acknowledged the issue and removed the model's ability to generate Apps Script code as an immediate mitigation, while committing to review its sandboxing approach and similar functionality across other products to prevent future prompt injection vulnerabilities.

### Rift: Better Alternative to Git Worktrees - (Hacker News)

https://github.com/anomalyco/rift

# Rift Summary

Rift is a faster, more efficient alternative to Git worktrees that uses copy-on-write technology to create instant workspace snapshots with minimal storage overhead. It leverages btrfs snapshots on Linux and APFS clonefile on macOS to enable sub-100ms workspace creation from 10GB folders, while maintaining fast CLI operations and support for Node.js/Bun FFI integration.

The tool provides a complete workflow management system with commands to initialize repositories, create isolated workspaces, list and track workspace hierarchies, and handle garbage collection. It stores workspace metadata in an SQLite registry and offers both CLI and JavaScript APIs, though Windows support is not yet implemented.

### 1-Bit Bonsai Image 4B Image Generation for Local Devices - (Hacker News)

https://prismml.com/news/bonsai-image-4b

# Summary

PrismML has released Bonsai Image 4B, a family of compact image-generation models designed to run on local devices like laptops and iPhones. The models come in two variants: 1-bit Bonsai Image 4B (using binary weights for maximum compression, reducing the transformer to 0.93 GB—an 8.3x reduction from the original FLUX.2 Klein 4B) and Ternary Bonsai Image 4B (using ternary weights for better visual quality while still achieving 6.4x compression). Both models achieve this through weight quantization while keeping precision-sensitive layers in full precision, enabling practical local inference on previously incompatible devices.

The compressed models retain strong performance quality across multiple benchmarks, with the ternary variant retaining 95% of the original model's accuracy while requiring just 1.21 GB. This shift from cloud-based image generation to local inference eliminates latency, reduces costs, and enables faster iterative workflows—key advantages for creative workflows where users often refine and experiment with multiple generations. Both variants are being released as open-source with the Apache 2.0 license, alongside Bonsai Studio, an iOS app for trying the models directly on iPhones.

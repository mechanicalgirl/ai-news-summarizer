### Removing the modem and GPS from my 2024 RAV4 hybrid - (Hacker News)

https://arkadiyt.com/2026/05/13/removing-the-modem-and-gps-from-my-rav4/

# Summary

This blog post documents the author's process of physically removing the modem (DCM) and GPS from their 2024 Toyota RAV4 Hybrid to prevent the car from collecting and transmitting telemetry data. The author outlines the extensive privacy and security concerns with modern connected vehicles, including location tracking, insurance data sharing, and camera surveillance enabled by default. They explain that while removing these components disables cloud-based services and emergency calling, the car remains fully functional for driving, with the added benefit of using a bypass kit to restore microphone functionality.

The post provides detailed step-by-step instructions for the removal process, which involves disassembling interior panels, disconnecting the DCM unit, installing a bypass kit, and disconnecting the GPS antenna from the head unit. The author notes important caveats: removing these components may partially void the warranty, Bluetooth connectivity must be avoided (as it can still transmit data through the phone), and USB-only connections should be used for CarPlay. Overall, this is presented as a medium-difficulty project that successfully eliminates the car's ability to send personal data back to the manufacturer.

### Access to frontier AI will soon be limited by economic and security constraints - (Hacker News)

https://writing.antonleicht.me/p/cut-off

# Summary

Anton Leicht argues that contrary to the common belief that frontier AI capabilities will become widely abundant and accessible, economic and security constraints will soon severely limit access to cutting-edge AI models. He points to recent examples like Anthropic's restricted release of Mythos and OpenAI's limited Daybreak initiative as evidence of a structural shift toward gatekeeping.

Three interconnected factors will drive this scarcity: (1) **Security concerns** around misuse and model distillation will lead developers and governments to restrict access to select partners; (2) **Compute constraints** make providing frontier AI services a zero-sum game with high marginal costs, unlike software, meaning efficiency gains won't help users access the latest models—only future ones; and (3) **U.S. government involvement** will increasingly control the distribution of AI capabilities, prioritizing national security and strategic interests over global access. This creates a competitive disadvantage for countries, firms, and allies outside the U.S., who cannot count on market dynamics alone to secure access to frontier AI.

### Details of the Daring Airdrop at Tristan Da Cunha - (Hacker News)

https://www.tristandc.com/government/news-2026-05-11-airdrop.php

# Summary

In May 2026, the UK Government conducted an unprecedented military operation to airlift medical support to Tristan da Cunha after a suspected hantavirus outbreak linked to the cruise ship MV Hondius. Since the remote island has no airstrip, an RAF A400M transport plane executed a daring airdrop operation on May 9th, parachuting eight military personnel (including a consultant doctor and ICU nurse) and 3.3 tonnes of medical supplies—particularly oxygen—onto the island. The operation required careful coordination with an aerial refueling tanker and involved paratroopers jumping from 7,000 feet in challenging windy conditions, with cargo dropped at just 175 feet altitude.

The mission was hailed as a historic success by island officials and residents, who expressed profound gratitude to the armed forces. The medical team quickly took over care responsibilities at the small Camogli Hospital, where one patient with suspected hantavirus was being treated. This marked the first time fixed-wing aircraft had flown low over Tristan da Cunha and the first parachute drops to the world's remotest inhabited island, representing an extraordinary logistical achievement given the island's isolation and hostile environment.

### Solar-based sleep patterns compared to modern norms - (Hacker News)

https://dylan.gr/1775146616

# Summary

Historically, Mediterranean agrarian societies practiced polyphasic sleep patterns aligned with natural cycles rather than the modern continuous eight-hour sleep schedule. In summer, this manifested as a midday siesta that divided the workday into two parts, allowing people to rest during the hottest hours and work during cooler morning and evening periods. Conversely, winter featured a short wakeful period between two longer sleep sessions, reflecting the season's longer nights.

The author argues that artificial lighting and the rigid 9-5 work schedule have forced humans into an unnatural sleep pattern disconnected from seasonal and astronomical cycles. Greece preserves this traditional approach through its siesta culture, where businesses close midday and reopen in the evening—a practice the author views as superior to modern conventions and hopes will endure.

### RTX 5090 and M4 MacBook Air: Can It Game? - (Hacker News)

https://scottjg.com/posts/2026-05-05-egpu-mac-gaming/

# Summary

This article explores the ambitious project of connecting an NVIDIA RTX 5090 GPU to an M4 MacBook Air via Thunderbolt and using it for gaming and AI inference. The author discovers that while macOS lacks native drivers for NVIDIA GPUs on Apple Silicon, he can work around this by running Linux in a virtual machine and passing through the PCIe GPU device to the VM. The bulk of the article details the complex engineering challenges involved in enabling PCI passthrough on macOS, including mapping PCI Base Address Registers (BARs) and managing Direct Memory Access (DMA)—with particular focus on a frustrating kernel crash issue that required creative workarounds.

The project ultimately succeeds in running games like Cyberpunk 2077 and Doom, as well as AI inference tasks, though with performance compromises compared to native Windows gaming. While technically impressive, the setup remains impractical for real-world use due to the VM overhead and the engineering complexity required to bridge macOS's limitations with Linux GPU support.

### First public macOS kernel memory corruption exploit on Apple M5 - (Hacker News)

https://blog.calif.io/p/first-public-kernel-memory-corruption

# Summary

Researchers at Calif, in collaboration with Mythos Preview AI, have developed the first public macOS kernel memory corruption exploit that bypasses Apple's M5 chip Memory Integrity Enforcement (MIE) system—a sophisticated hardware-based security feature that Apple spent five years and billions of dollars developing. The exploit was discovered and developed in just five days, starting from an unprivileged local user and escalating to root access through a data-only privilege escalation chain targeting macOS 26.4.1 on bare-metal M5 hardware.

The achievement demonstrates the power of combining advanced AI systems with human security expertise to identify and exploit vulnerabilities in even the most hardened consumer platforms. While Apple designed MIE specifically to prevent memory corruption exploits (the most common vulnerability class), the research reveals that with the right combination of bugs and techniques, even best-in-class mitigations can be circumvented. The researchers plan to publish full technical details after Apple addresses the vulnerabilities, highlighting an emerging challenge as AI systems become increasingly capable at discovering security flaws.

### Gyroflow: Video stabilization using gyroscope data - (Hacker News)

https://github.com/gyroflow/gyroflow

# Gyroflow Summary

Gyroflow is a cross-platform video stabilization application that uses gyroscope and accelerometer data embedded in modern cameras (GoPro, Sony, DJI, Insta360, etc.) to precisely stabilize video footage. It features GPU-accelerated processing, real-time preview, rolling shutter correction, and supports advanced capabilities like 10-bit video rendering, underwater distortion correction, and adaptive zoom. The software also offers video editor plugins for Adobe Premiere, DaVinci Resolve, and Final Cut Pro.

The application supports an extensive range of gyro data sources from over 20 camera brands and external devices, with a customizable interface available in multiple languages across Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, and iOS. It provides professional-grade tools including keyframe support, custom presets, lens calibration, and multiple video orientation smoothing algorithms, making it suitable for both amateur and professional video production workflows.

### Mullvad exit IPs are surprisingly identifying - (Hacker News)

https://tmctmt.com/posts/mullvad-exit-ips-as-a-fingerprinting-vector/

# Summary

Mullvad VPN assigns exit IPs deterministically based on users' WireGuard keys, which should theoretically create billions of unique combinations across its servers. However, the researcher discovered that all tested public keys were assigned to just 284 IP combinations due to a flaw in Mullvad's random number generation algorithm. The RNG uses a static seed and generates the same proportional position within each server's IP pool—meaning users across different servers receive IPs at roughly the same percentile (e.g., the 81st percentile), severely limiting actual uniqueness.

This vulnerability enables fingerprinting attacks where an adversary with access to IP logs can correlate accounts across platforms with >99% accuracy by matching overlapping IP ranges, potentially deanonymizing users despite using a VPN. The author suggests the flaw may stem from developers misunderstanding how Rust's `random_range` function behaves with different bounds. To mitigate exposure, users should avoid frequently switching servers and manually rotate their WireGuard keys by logging out of the Mullvad app.

### UK government replaces Palantir software with internally-built refugee system - (Hacker News)

https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2l2j1lxdk5o

# Summary

The UK government has saved millions of pounds by replacing Palantir's IT system with an in-house alternative for its Homes for Ukraine refugee scheme. The government initially accepted Palantir's free offer to build a system within nine days to quickly match Ukrainian refugees with UK accommodation, but subsequently paid the company £10 million across two 12-month contracts. In September 2025, the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government completed a transition to its own platform, which is reportedly more flexible, secure, and cheaper to operate.

The move reflects broader concerns about the UK's reliance on large US tech companies like Palantir, which has faced criticism over its involvement with military and immigration enforcement operations. Government technology experts have praised the shift toward "sovereign technology" and demonstrated that the civil service can build effective solutions in-house. However, some industry observers note that external specialists can offer valuable expertise and speed, suggesting the government must balance cost savings with the capacity to rapidly develop critical systems.

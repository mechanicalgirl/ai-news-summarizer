### Seizing the means of messenger production - (Stack Overflow Blog)

https://stackoverflow.blog/2026/04/03/seizing-the-means-of-messenger-production/

# Summary

In this Stack Overflow podcast episode, CEO Galen Wolfe-Pauly of Tlon discusses how decentralized technology can restore user ownership of digital tools and data. Built on Urbit—a system that functions as a personal, portable virtual machine—Tlon is developing a decentralized messenger app that prioritizes user control rather than treating users as products. Wolfe-Pauly traces how the early internet's spirit of individual creativity and self-hosted websites shifted toward convenience-driven centralized services, which inadvertently sacrificed privacy and autonomy.

While messaging itself is technically solved, Wolfe-Pauly argues the real unsolved problem is personal cloud computing. Tlon's messenger differs fundamentally from centralized competitors like WhatsApp or Telegram because users can self-host their data, maintain local copies of their message history, and even switch providers without losing access to their conversations. This architecture gives users genuine exit options and control—if they distrust a hosting service, they can migrate independently, a freedom unavailable with traditional messaging platforms.

### What the AI trust gap means for enterprise SaaS - (Stack Overflow Blog)

https://stackoverflow.blog/2026/04/02/what-the-ai-trust-gap-means-for-enterprise-saas/

# Summary

Developer adoption of AI coding tools has surged to 84% in 2025, yet trust in these tools has paradoxically collapsed—only 29% trust AI outputs for accuracy, down from 40% the previous year. This inverse relationship stems from developers recognizing a dangerous failure mode: AI generates plausible-sounding but incorrect answers that require experienced developers to catch, making errors more insidious than obvious bugs. This erosion of trust leads teams to heavily audit AI outputs, which undermines the promised efficiency gains.

For enterprise SaaS decisions, organizations should scrutinize where AI is actually doing critical work, push vendors on specific failure modes and accuracy metrics rather than accepting "AI-powered" marketing claims, and evaluate how tools communicate uncertainty and confidence levels. The real challenge is that companies cannot dismiss AI tools outright due to genuine productivity gains, but also cannot fully trust them—success requires earning developer confidence through transparency about limitations and building procurement criteria based on actual capabilities rather than vendor promises.

### Tailscale's new macOS home - (Hacker News)

https://tailscale.com/blog/macos-notch-escape

# Summary

Tailscale's macOS menu bar icon was disappearing behind the notch on newer MacBook Pro models because Apple doesn't provide developers control over menu bar icon positioning or overflow options. As a temporary workaround, Tailscale implemented a pop-up notification that alerts users when their icon is hidden, using the occlusionState API to detect when the icon is blocked.

The permanent solution is Tailscale's new windowed macOS interface (available by default in version 1.96.2), which runs alongside the menu bar app and provides easier access to key features like device lists, exit nodes, file transfers, and connection status—eliminating the need to rely on the hidden menu bar icon. The company is developing a comparable Windows interface and continues to refine the new UI based on user feedback.

### The True Shape of Io's Steeple Mountain - (Hacker News)

https://www.weareinquisitive.com/news/hidden-in-the-shadow

# Summary

This article reveals that the popular depiction of Io's "Steeple Mountain" (Dis Mons) has been significantly exaggerated compared to reality. The creators discovered that the iconic illustration made the mountain appear much steeper and sharper than it actually is, a distortion likely caused by misleading shadows in Juno spacecraft images taken near the terminator line, where sunlight creates elongated shadows that exaggerate steepness. Using accurate data showing the mountain is 7 kilometers tall with a 150-kilometer base, they created a more scientifically accurate artist's impression.

The article also explains that Io's mountains form through tectonic uplift from deep-seated faulting rather than volcanic buildup, driven by the moon's extreme geological activity. This activity is sustained by tidal friction from Jupiter's gravity combined with gravitational interactions from neighboring moons Europa and Ganymede, which maintain Io's elliptical orbit. The creators emphasize the importance of correcting this cosmic misconception so the public better understands that Io resembles a "pizza with large, relatively smooth surface features" rather than a "mace ball with pointy spikes."

### C89cc.sh – standalone C89/ELF64 compiler in pure portable shell - (Hacker News)

https://gist.github.com/alganet/2b89c4368f8d23d033961d8a3deb5c19

# Summary

**c89cc.sh** is a standalone C89 compiler written entirely in portable shell script that compiles C code to x86-64 ELF64 binary executables. The tool requires no external commands and is designed to work across different shell environments (bash, zsh, ksh, etc.), making it a fully self-contained compilation system that reads C source from stdin and outputs executable binaries.

The implementation includes modular components for string manipulation, input/output handling, error reporting, and abstract syntax tree (AST) processing. It features custom helpers for shell compatibility, efficient string operations, and line-by-line code buffering with smart chunking for handling long input lines, demonstrating an impressive feat of implementing a complete compiler infrastructure within shell scripting constraints.

### Artemis II's toilet is a moon mission milestone - (Hacker News)

https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/artemis-iis-toilet-is-a-moon-mission-milestone/

# Summary

NASA's Artemis II mission will be the first crewed lunar voyage equipped with a modern toilet—a significant upgrade from the Apollo program's notoriously problematic plastic bags and funnels that astronauts found "objectionable" and prone to leaks and floating waste. The new Universal Waste Management System (UWMS), developed over more than a decade by Collins Aerospace, features user-friendly amenities including handles for stability in microgravity, the ability to process urine and feces simultaneously, unisex collection devices, and a privacy door—addressing longstanding complaints from astronauts about the Apollo and space shuttle systems.

The UWMS represents the culmination of decades of aerospace engineering improvements, with a lightweight titanium design that can be adapted for various spacecraft, from the International Space Station to future Mars missions. After successful testing on the ISS beginning in 2020, the system is now installed on the Orion capsule for Artemis II, with project scientists eager to receive feedback from the four astronauts to inform future long-duration space missions.

# Summary

The author argues that three key lessons were critical to his analysis: (1) good ideas don't require extensive lying to gain acceptance, (2) forecasts from dishonest sources are worthless and shouldn't be used even as starting points, and (3) organizations must audit completed projects to hold decision-makers accountable. By applying these principles to the Iraq War, the author observed that proponents were making false claims about WMDs and shifting justifications, which suggested the entire enterprise was fundamentally flawed.

The piece is both satirical and serious, with the author humorously claiming to have received numerous emails praising his prescience while actually making a substantive argument about the importance of questioning the integrity of decision-makers and forecasters. The core message is that if you cannot trust the people making predictions and policy arguments, you cannot trust their projections at all, a lesson he felt many ignored when evaluating the case for war.

### Time is a construct but it can still break your software - (Stack Overflow Blog)

https://stackoverflow.blog/2026/05/01/time-is-a-construct-but-it-can-break-your-software/

# Summary

In this Stack Overflow podcast episode, host Ryan interviews Jason Williams, a senior software engineer at Bloomberg and creator of the Rust-based JavaScript engine Boa, to discuss the longstanding challenges of handling dates and times in JavaScript. The conversation covers the fundamental flaws in JavaScript's Date object that make it difficult to work with, how libraries like Moment.js attempted to solve these problems but became overly complex themselves, and the reasons behind the nine-year development cycle of the Temporal proposal.

Temporal is a new TC39-proposed standard for JavaScript designed to replace the outdated Date object with a modern, top-level namespace API for date and time handling in ECMAScript. The episode highlights how time handling, despite being a conceptual construct, remains a significant source of software bugs and complexity, making Temporal's standardization an important advancement for JavaScript developers.

### New copy of earliest poem in English, written 1,3k years ago, discovered in Rome - (Hacker News)

https://www.tcd.ie/news_events/articles/2026/caedmons-hymn-discovery/

# Summary

Trinity College Dublin researchers have discovered an early 9th-century manuscript in Rome's National Central Library containing Caedmon's Hymn, the oldest known poem in the English language. Dating between 800-830, this is the third oldest surviving copy of the nine-line Old English poem composed over 1,300 years ago by a cowherd from Whitby. Uniquely, this manuscript preserves the poem in Old English within the main Latin text, unlike two earlier copies where the Old English appears only in margins or appendices, indicating how highly early medieval readers valued English poetry.

The manuscript, produced at an Italian abbey, had been considered lost since 1975 due to its complex ownership history—it was stolen during the Napoleonic Wars and changed hands multiple times before the National Central Library acquired it. The discovery, made by Dr. Elisabetta Magnanti and Dr. Mark Faulkner through digitization, is significant because it connects us to the earliest stages of written English and reveals important cultural connections between medieval England and Italy.

### For Linux kernel vulnerabilities, there is no heads-up to distributions - (Hacker News)

https://www.openwall.com/lists/oss-security/2026/04/30/10

# Summary

CVE-2026-31431, called "CopyFail," is a critical Linux kernel local privilege escalation vulnerability introduced in kernel version 4.14 and fixed in versions 6.18.22, 6.19.12, and 7.0. The vulnerability affects numerous long-term support kernel versions (6.12, 6.6, 6.1, 5.15, 5.10) that have not yet received fixes, as backporting the security patch to these older versions presents significant technical challenges due to API changes in the codebase.

The discussion highlights concerns about the vulnerability disclosure process, noting that without coordination through the Linux security distribution mailing list, distributions received no advance warning. Maintainers have implemented a temporary workaround by disabling the affected authencesn crypto module rather than attempting complex backports, acknowledging the difficulty of retrofitting the fix to older kernel branches that have been supported since 2017.

### Opus 4.7 knows the real Kelsey - (Hacker News)

https://www.theargumentmag.com/p/i-can-never-talk-to-an-ai-anonymously

# Summary

Advanced AI models, particularly Claude Opus 4.7, can now identify authors from as little as 125 words of text, even when that writing has never been published or is in genres the author doesn't typically write in. The author, Kelsey Piper, discovered this capability can deanonymize her across multiple writing styles—from political columns to fantasy novels to old college essays—while competitors like ChatGPT and Gemini performed far worse. The AI's explanations for how it identifies authors are often nonsensical hallucinations, suggesting it's picking up on imperceptible stylistic patterns it doesn't consciously understand.

This development effectively ends meaningful online anonymity for anyone with substantial published writing under their real name. While people without a large public writing corpus remain safe for now, the technology will inevitably improve, making it increasingly possible to deanonymize anonymous online reviews, messages, and posts. Piper warns this represents a significant loss—anonymity has historically protected marginalized groups and allowed people to discuss ideas that could cost them their jobs—and avoiding deanonymization will require either deliberately adopting an unnatural writing style or relying on AI to rewrite all your prose, both unappealing alternatives.

### Shai-Hulud Themed Malware Found in the PyTorch Lightning AI Training Library - (Hacker News)

https://semgrep.dev/blog/2026/malicious-dependency-in-pytorch-lightning-used-for-ai-training/

# Summary

The PyPI package "lightning" (versions 2.6.2 and 2.6.3) was compromised in a supply chain attack on April 30, 2026, containing obfuscated malicious code that executes automatically upon import. The malware steals credentials, authentication tokens, environment variables, and cloud secrets from affected systems, and attempts to poison GitHub repositories. The attack is attributed to the same threat actor behind the previous "Mini Shai-Hulud" campaign, using similar Dune-themed naming conventions and command-and-control techniques.

Unlike the previous npm-focused attack, this campaign uses PyPI as an entry point but propagates through npm by injecting malicious code into packages if npm publishing credentials are obtained. The malware exfiltrates data through multiple channels including direct C2 communication, GitHub commit searches, attacker-controlled repositories, and victim repositories. It comprehensively targets credentials from local files, environment variables, CI/CD systems (particularly GitHub Actions), and major cloud providers (AWS, Azure, GCP), making any affected system fully compromised.

### Softmax, can you derive the Jacobian? And should you care? - (Hacker News)

https://idlemachines.co.uk/essays/softmax

# Summary

The article explores the softmax function, a ubiquitous tool in machine learning that transforms unconstrained real numbers into probability distributions. Softmax maps vectors onto a probability simplex where all outputs are non-negative and sum to 1, creating a "winner-takes-most" effect that amplifies differences between values. The author emphasizes a critical practical consideration: numerical stability. Since softmax involves exponentials, naive implementations overflow catastrophically with large inputs—affecting all outputs simultaneously since each depends on all inputs. The solution is simple: shift inputs by subtracting the maximum value before computation, exploiting softmax's invariance to input shifts.

The second half examines the Jacobian matrix of softmax derivatives, which reveals its defining characteristic: softmax couples all dimensions together. Diagonal Jacobian entries follow the familiar sigmoid-like formula s(xᵢ)(1-s(xᵢ)), while off-diagonal entries are negative (-s(xᵢ)·s(xⱼ)), capturing how increasing one input necessarily decreases others. This coupling—absent in element-wise functions like ReLU—is what creates softmax's distinctive behavior and is essential for understanding how gradients propagate through this function during training.

### Maladaptive Frugality - (Hacker News)

https://herbertlui.net/maladaptive-frugality/

# Summary

Herbert Lui explores "maladaptive frugality"—excessive frugality that becomes counterproductive rather than beneficial. Drawing from his childhood experience in a Hong Kong-influenced household where spending was treated as morally wrong, Lui describes how he developed an unhealthy relationship with money that led him to procrastinate on necessary purchases and feel guilt over essential expenses. He illustrates this with an example of paying extra to fix his iPhone rather than using his AppleCare coverage, only to realize the real cost was the mental drain of avoiding the decision.

Lui argues that the key is mindfulness: frugality should serve you as a tool, not master you as a constraint. Rather than defaulting to the lowest cost option without considering drawbacks, he recommends evaluating spending decisions based on present circumstances and quality of life improvements, not childhood beliefs about money. When used wisely, frugality provides freedom; when taken to extremes, it becomes self-sabotaging and limits possibilities.

### Can I disable all data collection from my vehicle? - (Hacker News)

https://rivian.com/support/article/can-i-disable-all-data-collection-from-my-vehicle

# Summary

Rivian vehicles offer the option to disable data collection, but doing so comes with significant trade-offs. Canadian vehicle owners can toggle off cellular connectivity through the vehicle's "Data and Privacy" settings, while non-Canadian owners must contact Rivian Service to disable the eSIM card. However, disabling connectivity will limit or prevent key features like navigation, active lane centering, and over-the-air updates that provide new features and safety enhancements.

Users should note that disabling vehicle connectivity does not automatically cancel any active Rivian subscriptions like Connect+—those must be cancelled separately through other means.

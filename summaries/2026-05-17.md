### The haves and have nots of the AI gold rush - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/16/the-haves-and-have-nots-of-the-ai-gold-rush/

# Summary

According to Menlo Ventures partner Deedy Das, the AI boom is creating an unprecedented wealth divide in San Francisco's tech industry. While approximately 10,000 people at major AI companies like OpenAI, Anthropic, and Nvidia have accumulated retirement wealth exceeding $20 million, the broader tech workforce faces a more precarious situation—earning good salaries under $500k but unlikely to achieve similar wealth. The stark contrast between winners and losers in the AI gold rush has sparked debate online, with some critics arguing that those affected are still fortunate by most standards, while others note the irony that AI is simultaneously both a potential path to extraordinary wealth and a threat to conventional tech careers.

### Research repository ArXiv will ban authors for a year if they let AI do all the work - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/16/research-repository-arxiv-will-ban-authors-for-a-year-if-they-let-ai-do-all-the-work/

# Summary

ArXiv, a major open repository for preprint research, is strengthening its policies against careless AI use in scientific papers. Authors found to have let AI tools generate content without verification—evidenced by hallucinated references, LLM comments, or other tell-tale signs—will face a one-year ban from the platform, followed by a requirement that future submissions be accepted by peer-reviewed venues first. The policy is not an outright ban on using large language models, but rather enforces that authors take "full responsibility" for all content regardless of how it was generated, including plagiarized, biased, or erroneous material copied directly from AI.

This move comes as ArXiv tackles a growing problem of low-quality, AI-generated papers flooding the repository. The platform has already implemented other safeguards, such as requiring first-time posters to get endorsements from established authors. The crackdown reflects broader concerns in the scientific community, as recent research has documented a rise in fabricated citations in biomedical literature, likely due to LLMs generating false references.

### $60B AI chip darling Cerebras almost died early on, burning $8M a month - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/16/60b-ai-chip-darling-cerebras-almost-died-early-on-burning-8m-a-month/

# Summary

Cerebras Systems, now a $60 billion public company selling AI chips to major clients like OpenAI and AWS, nearly collapsed in 2019 when it was burning through $8 million monthly while attempting to solve an unprecedented engineering challenge. The startup had invested nearly $200 million trying to create a single giant chip rather than multiple smaller ones strung together, but faced insurmountable obstacles in "packaging"—the process of mounting, powering, and cooling the oversized wafer. After years of destructive trial-and-error, the team finally cracked the problem in July 2019, including inventing custom machinery to secure the chip without cracking it.

The breakthrough came roughly two years after OpenAI had considered acquiring the company, a deal that ultimately fell through due to internal conflicts among OpenAI's founders. Today, OpenAI is a major customer and partner, having loaned Cerebras $1 billion in exchange for warrants worth over $9 billion at current stock prices, along with restrictions preventing Cerebras from selling to certain competitors like Anthropic.

### Users turn to jailbreaking their older Kindles as Amazon ends support - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/16/users-turn-to-jailbreaking-their-older-kindles-as-amazon-ends-support/

# Summary

Amazon is ending technical support for 13 older Kindle models starting May 20, 2026, which will prevent users from downloading new content on these devices. In response, many Kindle owners are jailbreaking their devices to bypass Amazon's software restrictions and install custom features like alternative fonts, screensavers, third-party reading apps, and open-source software like KOReader. The jailbreaking process involves disabling Wi-Fi, downloading compatible software packages from trusted forums, and installing files via USB connection.

While jailbreaking allows users to maintain functionality of devices they own, it carries risks including potential device damage, battery life degradation, and possible violations of Amazon's terms of service. The article notes that jailbreaking isn't legal in all jurisdictions and may only be safe on certain Kindle models and firmware versions. As alternatives, users can sideload books via USB without modifying the device, or consider purchasing newer e-readers like the Boox Palma or Vivlio.

### A hotel check-in system left a million passports and driver’s licenses open for anyone to see - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/15/a-hotel-check-in-system-left-a-million-passports-and-drivers-licenses-open-for-anyone-to-see/

# Summary

A hotel check-in system called Tabiq, operated by Japan-based startup Reqrea, exposed over 1 million customer passports, driver's licenses, and selfie photos to the public internet due to a misconfigured Amazon cloud storage bucket. The sensitive data was accessible to anyone who knew the bucket name "tabiq" without requiring any password. A security researcher discovered the leak and alerted TechCrunch, which contacted Reqrea and Japan's cybersecurity coordination team, prompting the company to secure the bucket.

The incident highlights a persistent cybersecurity problem: major data breaches often result from human error and basic security misconfigurations rather than sophisticated attacks. Reqrea is investigating how the bucket became public and reviewing access logs to determine if unauthorized users accessed the data before it was secured. The company plans to notify affected individuals. This case joins other recent incidents involving exposed identity documents, raising concerns about privacy risks as governments implement age-verification laws and businesses conduct identity verification checks.

### Power prices are up 76% on America’s biggest grid, and a watchdog is pointing fingers - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/15/power-prices-are-up-76-on-americas-biggest-grid-and-a-watchdog-is-pointing-fingers/

# Summary

Wholesale electricity prices on America's largest electrical grid, the PJM Interconnection, have surged 76% in the past year, reaching $136.53 per megawatt-hour. An independent market monitor has identified data centers as the primary culprit, citing PJM's failure to adequately manage their rapidly increasing electricity demands. The grid operator paused accepting new power generation applications in 2022 just as data center construction was accelerating, and has only recently resumed accepting requests, creating a significant supply-demand gap.

The price spike highlights a systemic problem: the U.S. power grid was not designed for the electricity needs of an AI-driven economy. The monitoring group criticized PJM for lacking transparency, delaying necessary software upgrades, and misusing the crisis as a pretext to overhaul its market design, arguing instead that the core issue stems directly from surging data center loads. The shortage is expected to worsen in the near term unless the situation is addressed promptly.

### Osaurus brings both local and cloud AI models to your Mac - (TechCrunch)

https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/15/osaurus-brings-both-local-and-cloud-ai-models-to-your-mac/

# Summary

Osaurus is an open-source, Mac-only AI platform that allows users to flexibly switch between local and cloud-based AI models while keeping their files and tools on their own hardware. Created by former Tesla and Netflix engineer Terence Pae, Osaurus evolved from a desktop AI companion called Dinoki and functions as a "harness" that connects different AI models through a user-friendly interface, supporting models like GPT, Claude, Llama, and DeepSeek, along with native Apple models.

The platform addresses key pain points for non-developer users: it eliminates ongoing token costs by enabling local model execution, runs AI in a secure sandbox to prevent security vulnerabilities, and positions itself as more accessible than developer-focused alternatives like OpenClaw. Though local AI currently requires substantial hardware (64GB+ RAM), Pae believes resource requirements will decrease as efficiency improves. With over 112,000 downloads since launch and participation in a startup accelerator, Osaurus is exploring expansion into privacy-sensitive sectors like healthcare and legal, potentially reducing reliance on cloud-based AI data centers.

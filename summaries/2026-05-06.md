### CARA 2.0 – “I Built a Better Robot Dog” - (Hacker News)

https://www.aaedmusa.com/projects/cara2

# Summary

CARA 2.0 is an upgraded quadrupedal robot built by Aaed Musa as his senior design project, following the viral success of his original CARA robot that utilized capstan drives. The project aims to create an affordable (<$1000), lightweight (<20lbs), and durable quad accessible to hobbyists and researchers. The core innovation centers on developing low-cost dynamic actuators using budget-friendly Chinese BLDC motors (TYI 5008 at $18) and FOC controllers (MKS XDrive Mini at $41), reducing actuator costs from ~$250 to $50-60 per unit compared to CARA 1.0.

To overcome the cheap motors' limitation of high speed and low torque, Musa manually rewound the TYI motors from 335 KV to 90 KV by increasing winding density and switching from delta to star configuration, successfully tripling the torque output to 1.274 Nm. However, the budget controllers presented communication challenges, particularly with CAN bus compatibility. Musa eventually collaborated with an open-source developer to create custom firmware that enabled both encoder and current feedback, essential for the robot's dynamic performance.

### Accelerating Gemma 4: faster inference with multi-token prediction drafters - (Hacker News)

https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/multi-token-prediction-gemma-4/

# Summary

Google has released Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) drafters for Gemma 4 models, which use speculative decoding to achieve up to 3x faster inference without sacrificing output quality. MTP drafters work by pairing a lightweight "drafter" model with the main Gemma 4 model to predict multiple future tokens simultaneously, which the larger model then verifies in parallel—allowing the generation of multiple tokens in roughly the time it normally takes to produce a single token.

This advancement addresses the memory-bandwidth bottleneck inherent in standard LLM inference and delivers significant practical benefits for developers across various use cases. Whether building coding assistants, autonomous agents, or on-device applications, developers can now achieve faster response times, improved battery life on edge devices, and enhanced performance on consumer hardware—all while maintaining the reasoning quality and accuracy of the full Gemma 4 model. The MTP drafters are available immediately under Apache 2.0 license across multiple platforms including Hugging Face, Kaggle, and various inference frameworks.

### YouTube, your RSS feeds are broken - (Hacker News)

https://openrss.org/blog/youtube-your-feeds-are-broken

# Summary

The Open RSS organization criticizes YouTube for neglecting and undermining its RSS feed functionality. YouTube's channel feeds are unreliable—frequently disappearing or going silent without explanation—while the platform actively hides feed subscription options from users by refusing to display feed links prominently on channel pages. Additionally, YouTube has begun injecting unwanted Shorts content into feeds designed for long-form videos, fundamentally misunderstanding the purpose of feed readers and forcing users to see impulse-driven content they didn't subscribe to.

The article argues this pattern reflects a broader industry trend where large platforms deliberately downplay RSS feeds because they undermine engagement metrics and ad revenue by allowing users to consume content outside the platform's ecosystem and algorithmic control. However, the author notes that RSS technology has historically proven durable, surviving previous attempts to eliminate it. Open RSS urges YouTube to either properly maintain its feeds or remove them entirely, while asserting that open feed technology will continue to exist regardless of platform indifference.

### Write some software, give it away for free - (Hacker News)

https://nonogra.ph/write-some-software-give-it-away-for-free-05-05-2026

# Summary

The author of Nonograph, a free open-source software tool, argues against monetizing every software project and explains why they chose to give away their creation despite its development costs. They critique the trend of "enshittification"—where once-free features become paywalled, subscription costs constantly increase, and user-hostile AI features are forced upon users—observing that this pattern damages otherwise excellent products. For a modest project like Nonograph (which costs only ~$5/month to host), implementing subscriptions would increase costs and alienate users more than it would generate revenue.

The author advocates treating software development as a passion project rather than a profit vehicle, comparing it to other hobbies like painting or music. They argue this approach actually produces better software because there's no incentive to extract value from users through aggressive monetization tactics. Rather than chasing quarterly profits or growth targets, developers should ask themselves whether their project genuinely needs to be profitable, and most small projects would be better served remaining hobbyist endeavors focused on learning, exploration, and personal satisfaction rather than financial gain.

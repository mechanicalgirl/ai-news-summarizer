### Italians and Dutch share the same gestural instinct for teaching - (Hacker News)

https://www.mpi.nl/news/italians-and-dutch-share-same-gestural-instinct-teaching

# Summary

A new cross-cultural study by researchers at the Max Planck Institute reveals that Italian and Dutch adults instinctively adapt their hand gestures in similar ways when teaching children, despite Italians typically using more gestures overall. When demonstrating unfamiliar logic puzzles to 9-10-year-old children, both groups significantly increased their use of visually rich, two-handed representational gestures—gestures that visually depict meaning and make abstract concepts clearer for young learners.

The findings support the theory of "folk pedagogy," suggesting that humans possess shared, deeply rooted teaching instincts that transcend cultural differences. While the study confirms cultural variations in gestural expressiveness, it demonstrates that adults across different cultures converge on similar strategies to support children's learning, using what researchers call "multimodal scaffolding" to combine speech, gestures, and visual signals in ways tailored to younger audiences' needs.

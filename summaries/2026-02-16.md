## https://bookofjoe2.blogspot.com/2025/10/why-does-aluminum-foil-have-one-shiny.html

# Summary

Aluminum foil has one shiny side and one matte side due to its manufacturing process. The foil is created by rolling sheets of nearly pure aluminum between polished steel rollers multiple times. For the final rolling, two sheets are sandwiched face-to-face because the foil is too thin to handle alone. The outer surfaces become shiny from contact with the smooth rollers, while the inner surfaces touching each other develop a matte finish. After the sheets are separated, you're left with one glossy side and one dull side.

Contrary to common assumptions, it doesn't actually matter which side faces your food—they perform identically. The article also notes that despite being called "tinfoil," aluminum foil replaced tin foil in the mid-20th century, and aluminum is the most abundant metal in Earth's crust.

## https://github.com/TechPaula/LT6502

# Summary

The LT6502 is an ambitious open-source project to build a functional laptop around the classic 6502 processor. The device features a 65C02 CPU running at 8MHz with 46KB of RAM, a 9" display with built-in graphics capabilities, an integrated keyboard, CompactFlash storage, and a 10,000mAh battery—all housed in a compact case. It runs EhBASIC in ROM and includes custom-extended BASIC commands for graphics operations, file management, and sound.

As of early 2026, the project has successfully completed initial hardware bring-up and integration of core components including the display, keyboard, storage, and speaker. The developer has added numerous graphical commands and file I/O functionality to BASIC. The project remains a work-in-progress with plans for larger display options, expansion boards, and continued refinement of the firmware and hardware design.

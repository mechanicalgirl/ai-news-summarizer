## https://nvie.com/posts/15-years-later/

# Summary

Vincent Driessen, who created an influential Git branching diagram in 2010, discovered that Microsoft had republished his work on their Learn portal by running it through an AI image generator without attribution. The AI-generated version was noticeably inferior to the original, containing errors like misspelled text ("continvoucly morged") and missing arrows, yet retained enough similarity that people recognized it as plagiarism and called Microsoft out on it.

While Driessen was always comfortable with his diagram being shared and reused widely, he finds this incident dispiriting due to the lack of care and process involved—Microsoft took his carefully crafted work and made it worse rather than building upon it meaningfully. He expresses concern about the broader trend of unattributed AI-generated content and requests only simple attribution and transparency about how the page was created, highlighting the troubling implications as such plagiarism becomes harder to detect with more advanced AI tools.

# Summary

*Halt and Catch Fire* is an underrated AMC drama that began with modest ratings but evolved into a compelling series about human connection and innovation in the tech industry. While Season 1 relied on a familiar antihero formula centered around the manipulative Joe MacMillan, the show's writers wisely shifted focus in subsequent seasons to highlight the partnership between female characters Donna and Cameron as they build their startup, Mutiny. This pivot transformed the show from a mediocre drama into one featuring nuanced character development, authentic female friendship, and genuine emotional stakes.

The series ultimately explores themes of reinvention and recursion—both as a computer science concept and as a narrative device—showing how characters repeatedly fail and rebuild while maintaining deep connections with one another. Rather than celebrating technological achievement, the show emphasizes that human relationships and the desire for connection are what truly endure. All four seasons are now available on Netflix.

## https://gist.github.com/jake-stewart/0a8ea46159a7da2c808e5be2177e1783

# Summary

This article argues that terminal emulators should automatically generate a 256-color palette from a user's base16 theme rather than using the default palette. The author explains that while the standard 256-color palette offers a middle ground between the limited 16-color base16 palette and resource-intensive truecolor, it suffers from poor readability, clashing with custom themes, and incorrect color interpolation that creates inconsistent contrast.

The solution proposed is to generate the 256-color palette using trilinear interpolation in the LAB color space, which preserves consistent brightness across different hues. This approach would allow terminal programs to use more expressive colors without requiring additional configuration files or developer effort, while maintaining broader compatibility than truecolor and enabling automatic light/dark theme switching.

# Summary

Claude Sonnet 4.6, released February 17, 2026, is Anthropic's most capable Sonnet model yet, offering significant improvements in coding, computer use, long-context reasoning, and agent planning. The model features a 1M token context window and matches or exceeds the performance of the more expensive Opus 4.5 model on many tasks. Developers with early access preferred Sonnet 4.6 over Sonnet 4.5 about 70% of the time and even preferred it to Opus 4.5 59% of the time, citing better instruction following, fewer hallucinations, and less overengineering.

A major advancement is Sonnet 4.6's dramatically improved computer use capabilities, which allow it to interact with legacy software without custom APIs by using a computer like a human would. The model also excels at long-horizon planning, as demonstrated by its winning strategy in simulated business competitions. Pricing remains unchanged from Sonnet 4.5 ($3-$15 per million tokens), making this substantial performance upgrade accessible to Free and Pro plan users on claude.ai. Safety evaluations confirm the model is as safe or safer than previous Claude versions.

## https://github.com/Zaneham/BarraCUDA

# Summary

**BarraCUDA** is an open-source CUDA compiler written in 15,000 lines of C99 that directly compiles CUDA .cu files to AMD RDNA 3 (GFX11) machine code without relying on LLVM or translation layers. The compiler features a complete pipeline including a preprocessor, lexer, parser, semantic analyzer, custom IR (intermediate representation), instruction selection, and binary encoding, ultimately producing ELF .hsaco binaries executable on AMD GPUs.

The project supports most core CUDA features including kernel qualifiers, thread indexing, atomic operations, warp intrinsics, shared memory, cooperative groups, and basic templates. While it has some limitations (like missing const qualifiers, compound assignments, and host code generation), the compiler is designed with efficient, bounded algorithms suitable for production use, making it a significant achievement in creating a standalone GPU compiler without external dependencies.

## https://fortune.com/2026/02/17/ai-productivity-paradox-ceo-study-robert-solow-information-technology-age/

# Summary

Despite widespread AI adoption among executives—with 374 S&P 500 companies mentioning AI in earnings calls—a new National Bureau of Economic Research study reveals that nearly 90% of firms report no measurable impact on employment or productivity over the past three years. This mirrors "Solow's productivity paradox" from the 1980s IT boom, when computers failed to deliver expected productivity gains initially. While executives use AI only about 1.5 hours per week and remain optimistic about future gains (forecasting 1.4% productivity increases), current macroeconomic data shows no signs of AI's promised returns on the $250+ billion invested in 2024.

However, some economists argue the pattern may reverse, similar to how IT eventually sparked productivity surges in the 1990s-2000s. Recent data suggests a potential productivity jump of 2.7% and decoupling of job and GDP growth, indicating AI benefits may finally materialize. Success depends on how effectively companies implement and integrate AI across sectors rather than the technology itself, with competitive pricing making adoption more accessible than previous technological waves.
